The Nebenkostenabrechnung (operating cost statement) is one of the most important — and most error-prone — documents a German landlord produces each year. Get it wrong, and tenants can challenge it. Get it right, and you maintain trust and legal compliance.

## What is a Nebenkostenabrechnung?

It's the annual statement that breaks down the operating costs of a property and allocates them to each tenant based on agreed-upon distribution keys.

## Key deadlines

You must deliver the statement to tenants **within 12 months** after the billing period ends. Miss this deadline, and you lose the right to claim additional payments.

## Common allocation keys

- **Living area (m²)** — Most common for general costs
- **Number of persons** — Used for water, waste disposal
- **Unit count** — Equal split regardless of size
- **Consumption** — Based on meter readings (heating, water)

## Common mistakes to avoid

1. **Missing receipts** — Every cost must be backed by documentation
2. **Wrong allocation keys** — Must match what's in the lease
3. **Including non-allocable costs** — Repairs and maintenance are generally not allocable
4. **Late delivery** — The 12-month deadline is strict
